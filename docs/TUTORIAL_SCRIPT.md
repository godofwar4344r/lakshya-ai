# Lakshya AI · Tutorial Video Script

**Target length:** 3 minutes 30 seconds
**Style:** Clean screen recording with voiceover. No face cam needed. Background music: soft, building.
**Tool:** OBS Studio (free) or Loom for screen capture. Audacity for audio if you need to edit.

> Read the script naturally — don't sound like you're reading. Pause where you see `[breath]`. Record one full take, re-record any line that sounds off and splice in.

---

## SHOT 1 · Cold open (0:00–0:15)

**[On screen]** The Lakshya AI landing page hero — let the gradient animate for 2 seconds.

> *"This is Lakshya AI. The first AI literacy platform built for Indian institutions. In the next three minutes I'll show you how it works — for teachers, for students, and for the institutions that climb the national leaderboard."* [breath]

---

## SHOT 2 · The problem framing (0:15–0:35)

**[On screen]** Scroll down to the "three pillars" section.

> *"India has one and a half million schools. Fifty thousand colleges. And ninety-five percent of teachers have never written a single AI prompt. NEP 2020 mandates AI literacy, but there's no infrastructure to make it actually happen.* [breath] *Lakshya solves this by giving institutions three things in one platform."*

## SHOT 3 · Teacher Toolkit (0:35–1:30)

**[On screen]** Click "Get your institution on Lakshya" → land on `/login` → click "Enter as Teacher".

> *"This is Dr. Mishra. Physics teacher at IIMT University. She opens her toolkit and sees three AI tools. Let's use the lesson plan generator."*

**[On screen]** Click "Lesson Plan Generator". Type: Subject = Science, Grade = Class 8, Topic = Photosynthesis, Duration = 40.

> *"Class 8 Science, photosynthesis, forty minutes."*

**[On screen]** Click "Generate". Wait 2 seconds — let the streaming visibly start.

> *"Watch GPT-4o write a complete lesson plan in real time. Five-E pedagogy. Indian examples. NEP 2020 aligned. Built-in instruction on how Dr. Mishra can use AI live in the classroom. She has the entire lesson — including a quiz, an extension activity, and a differentiation tip — in under thirty seconds."* [breath]

**[On screen]** Let it stream for ~10 more seconds. Then scroll to show full output. Click "Copy" — show the copied state briefly.

> *"One click and it's in her clipboard, ready to print, edit, or paste into her LMS."*

## SHOT 4 · Student Arena (1:30–2:25)

**[On screen]** Navigate to `/student`. Land on the arena.

> *"Now switch roles. This is Arjun, a B-Tech student at IIMT. He sees four live AI challenges. Each one rewards XP for him and points for his institution. Let's open this one."*

**[On screen]** Click "Build a 5-prompt chain that teaches photosynthesis to a 7-year-old".

> *"Photosynthesis challenge. Hundred XP. Three day deadline. He writes a five-prompt chain — the kind of structured prompting he'd never have practiced if it wasn't a competition."*

**[On screen]** Paste in a 4–5 line sample submission. (Pre-write this so you don't fumble — see appendix below.)

> *"He hits submit."*

**[On screen]** Click submit. Wait for GPT-4o-mini to evaluate — should take ~2 seconds.

> *"Two seconds later — instant feedback. A score. Strengths. Improvements. A bonus tip for next time. And — this is the magic — IIMT University just moved up the national leaderboard because of his submission."*

## Appendix · Pre-written sample submission for SHOT 4

Paste this so the demo doesn't lose 30 seconds to you typing:

```
Prompt 1: "Pretend you are a friendly farmer. Tell a 7-year-old why your sunflowers always face the sun. Use no big words."
Prompt 2: "Now explain what the sunflower eats. Pretend its food comes from three magic ingredients."
Prompt 3: "Name the three magic ingredients and describe each one. Use a daily-life comparison for each."
Prompt 4: "Now invent a 4-line song the sunflower would sing about its three magic ingredients."
Prompt 5: "Finally, write a 1-paragraph story where a child meets the sunflower and learns photosynthesis through the song."
```

This submission will reliably score 75–85 from GPT-4o-mini, which keeps the demo upbeat.

## Recording checklist

- [ ] Close all other tabs (no distracting notifications)
- [ ] Browser zoom 110% for screen visibility
- [ ] Pre-load all routes once so the demo has no cold-start lag
- [ ] Have the sample submission in your clipboard before recording
- [ ] Record at 1080p minimum, 60fps if possible
- [ ] Export at H.264, target file size under 100MB

## Voiceover checklist

- [ ] Use a quiet room (closet works — clothes absorb echo)
- [ ] Phone earbuds with mic > laptop mic (cleaner audio)
- [ ] Speak 10% slower than feels natural
- [ ] Smile when reading — it audibly changes warmth
- [ ] Record one full take first to get rhythm, then splice corrections
